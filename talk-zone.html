Korean BL review

Soulmate

KoreaReflective romance
8.7

When we think of Japanese BLs, we often expect a soft, heartwarming story filled with subtle emotions, cute moments, and perhaps a confession or kiss by the end.
But Soulmate is different.
At its core, this isn't just a story about boys' love. It's a story about connection, destiny, and the way certain people unexpectedly enter our lives and slowly become irreplaceable. The series beautifully explores friendship, companionship, and the idea of finding someone who truly understands you.
What stood out to me was the cinematography and storytelling. Every scene feels intentional, allowing emotions to linger rather than rushing from one plot point to another.

That said, I did have one criticism. The series relies heavily on implications rather than clearly exploring the characters as a couple. While Episode 8 serves as a major turning point, I felt the story could have benefited from two additional episodes to further develop their relationship and explore their love more explicitly.
The emotional depth is undeniably one of the show's strengths, but at times the narrative wanders into side stories that take focus away from the central relationship.
Still, Soulmate is the kind of series you watch on a quiet afternoon with a cup of coffee and a few uninterrupted hours to yourself. It's gentle, reflective, and surprisingly moving.
Have you watched Soulmate? What did you think?
